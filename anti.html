What Are Antioxidants & How to Get More of Them





What Are Antioxidants?


Antioxidants are compounds found in foods that prevent oxidation in your body. Nutrients such as Vitamin A, Vitamin E, Vitamin C, and Selenium are all considered antioxidants. The oxidation process that damages cells is done by compounds called ‘free radicals’. Free radicals can occur for many reasons, both avoidable (smoking, air pollutants, toxin exposure, unhealthy food intake) and unavoidable (exercise, the ozone, x-rays). Even exercising can produce free radicals, however exercise can also increase total antioxidant capacity as well so it’s not an issue. Antioxidants neutralize these free radicals so that they cannot damage cells and cause longterm issues. That’s a great thing! So, keeping antioxidant levels high through eating these nutrient-rich foods can help support and protect our bodies.


How Else Do Antioxidants Benefit Us?


I like to refer to antioxidants as ‘recovery boosters’ - they supercharge your body and help you get back on your feet by preventing damage, lowering inflammation, and generally coming from some of our healthiest foods…which also boost recovery and sleep quality! By consuming more antioxidant-rich foods, we can provide our bodies with many vitamins and minerals as well to lower inflammation, promote recovery and prevent injury risk, support our immune systems, and even contribute to better sleep and quality of life. Sounds like something we all want. So, how can be incorporate more antioxidant-rich foods? Consume more foods that are antioxidant and nutrient-rich!


Where Do You Find Antioxidants?


Antioxidants can be found in a variety of fruits, vegetables, dark chocolate, and other plant foods. Sometimes these foods are referred to as ‘super foods’ due to their nutrient-rich content! They’re high in vitamins and minerals generally, as well as the antioxidant content. Some of these typical nutrient-rich food options that contain high amounts of antioxidants are listed below in no particular order.

  • Berries - blueberries, raspberries, strawberries, etc
  • Spinach
  • Kale
  • Pomegranate
  • Sweet cherries
  • Pecans
  • Prunes
  • Plums
  • Broccoli
  • Cabbage
  • Kale
  • Dark Chocolate
