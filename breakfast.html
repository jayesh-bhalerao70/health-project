Why Breakfast Is the Most Important Meal of the Day



I’m just gonna go ahead and say it — Breakfast really IS the most important meal of the day. In a world where we now glorify skipping breakfast and give it a fancy name (“intermittent fasting”), just hear me out. When it comes to being healthy, metabolism support is the key to success. Metabolism is another hot word right now in the health world. It describes your body’s ability to burn calories (aka utilize the food you eat) efficiently. You want your metabolism to be ‘high’, or working well, so that you can optimally utilize all the food you eat without seeing weight fluctuations.




What Impacts Metabolism?


Metabolism is impacted by the amount you eat as well as your hormones. The best way to ruin your metabolism is to constantly eat less than your body needs — otherwise known as dieting. Chronic dieting resets your metabolism too low as your body learns to adapt to the new, low level of calories. It gives your body a new, lower metabolism in order to learn to thrive with less fuel. So as you can imagine, the best way to increase metabolism or keep it working optimally is to eat the correct amount of food for you and prevent extended low calorie intake time periods. So, how does breakfast play into this?


How Breakfast Impacts Metabolism ?


Eating breakfast each day helps kickstart your metabolism when done correctly (stat tuned for the optimal breakfast combos). Eating within 1-2 hours of when you wake up in the morning helps your hormones and blood sugar stay balanced, which directly impact your hunger level throughout the entire rest of the day. When you skip breakfast, your body tends to feel that it needs to play ‘catch up’ and increases your hunger during the day. This typically causes you to eat more than if you would’ve just had breakfast in the first place! Research also shows that those who eat breakfast, particularly a high protein breakfast, tend to eat in a more balanced, portion controlled way throughout the day. This way of balanced and appropriate food intake leads to health longterm. The under eating and overeating cycle creates chaos instead of an optimized metabolism. So in order to optimize your metabolism and create a balanced way of eating each day, breakfast is key.




What Makes Up A Balanced Breakfast


A balanced breakfast and one that optimizes your metabolism is a high protein, macronutrient balanced breakfast. There are 3 macronutrients - carbs, protein, and fat. Choosing one from each category for your breakfast is a great place to start. Making sure you have at least 20 grams of protein is a great benchmark to shoot for to keep you fuller longer too. Protein and fat are responsible for signaling to your hormones that you are full and satisfied. With a carb-heavy breakfast, you won’t get that same feeling and inevitably your blood sugar won’t be balanced either. Protein and fat contribute to blood sugar balance and control when combined with carbs, where a carb-heavy breakfast with little protein or fat causes a blood sugar spike and crash later on.


Protein, carbs, and fat create a balance for both blood sugar control and the ability to keep you full and satisfied from your breakfast. Choose one from each category and start to notice how a healthy, balanced breakfast helps your entire day!
