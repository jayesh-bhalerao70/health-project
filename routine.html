How to Establish a Healthy Morning Routine





Why a Morning Routine is Important?


Even if you’re not a “morning person” per se, I think we can all admit there’s still some beauty in a fresh start, a new day, and new energy. And when it comes to living a healthy lifestyle, establishing a healthy morning routine can put you miles ahead in your day. If you already enjoy mornings, it may feel easier for you to make some of these adjustments to increase the healthy and focus of your mornings. If you’re someone who says a big '“NOPE” to mornings, I’ll remind you that health and feeling your best takes a little effort and is always worth the push to establish a habit. Nothing good happens without effort. So when we put first things first and start on the right foot, it actually makes things easier. Do the hard things first, even when you don’t want to, and the rest falls in place.


What Makes Up a Healthy Morning Routine?


Of course, we don’t just want any morning routine, we want a healthy one! We want a morning routine that furthers our health and goals we have and leaves us feeling ahead of the game for the day. So, what makes up a healthy morning routine? What should we focus on?

There are a few main focus areas for health to think about in your journey - metabolism support, hydration, movement, sleep, and stress management. The same is true with what we want to prioritize in our morning routine. Sleep is an obvious factor in the mornings since you will just have woken up. Most of us find mornings to be so hard because we don’t have a healthy sleep routine. And neglecting sleep can have many negative consequences for our health overall

After we’ve prioritized sleep, we want to tackle the other health areas in our morning routine. Movement, stress management, metabolism support, and hydration. While these can look very different for everyone, I recommend drinking at least 16 oz of water within 30 minutes of waking, having a well balanced breakfast, and getting in at least 15 minutes of movement (walking, stretching, yoga, HIIT, etc.) which helps your stress management as well. A well balanced breakfast should include a carbohydrate (fruit, oats, toast, potatoes, etc) and a protein source (eggs, beans, meat, protein powder, etc) for sure. It may also include healthy fats and veggies as well! This kickstarts your day and fuels your body well, preventing cravings and being overly hungry later in the day...aka helping you eat an appropriate amount each day.Don’t like breakfast? Try something small like a protein shake, smoothie or protein bar to get you used to the habit. Focusing on movement gets your blood flowing, releases tensions and eases stress and anxiety. Not to mention that some days you may feel like an even longer, more establish workout, which checks that box off your list for the day and frees up your evenings as well.

Morning routines are all about putting you a few steps ahead in your day in order to make you feel like you can conquer the world because you’ve already accomplished so much before the day truly even begins! They’re also about putting you a few steps ahead in your health, too, because healthy is all about consistency and habits that you can maintain overtime. Small, easy to implement habits in your morning routine help you seriously change your health in the long run!


Find What Works For You


The moral of the story here is to find what works for YOU while still prioritizing each of the wellness categories to create your optimal morning routine. Not everyone’s will be similar, but checking each of these boxes will leave you feeling energized, nourished, and ready to tackle your day while already prioritizing YOU. That’s a great feeling. It breeds motivation and confidence in your whole day. And who doesn’t want that?
